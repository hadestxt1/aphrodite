BORF Writing Contest Submission

A small story about making Base feel less far away.

Most people do not arrive through a specification, a roadmap, or a thread full of abbreviations. They arrive through a sentence that finally makes the thing feel understandable.

Editor’s note

This page treats BORF as a community voice: not a promise, not a pitch, but a way to make a fast-moving corner of the internet easier to read.

01 · First contact

The first barrier is not technical. It is language.

Someone sees Base mentioned in a post, a wallet, a mint, or a group chat. The page opens. The nouns arrive quickly. Layer 2. Gas. Bridge. Wallet. Contract. Onchain. None of these words are impossible, but together they can make a simple curiosity feel like homework.

BORF is interesting here because community writing can slow the room down. It can take one idea at a time, explain it without ceremony, and leave the reader with enough confidence to keep looking.

Clear writing is a kind of infrastructure. It gives people a place to stand before they decide where to go next.

Three notes

What a community page can do.

01

Name the room.

Explain where the reader is: Base is part of Ethereum’s wider landscape, built to make everyday experimentation feel lighter.

02

Remove the performance.

Good explainers do not need to sound advanced. They need to be exact, patient, and willing to use ordinary language.

03

Keep the human scale.

People learn through examples: a post, a screenshot, a small diagram, a friend saying what happened and why it mattered.

1

One useful explanation can do more than a hundred loud announcements.

Awareness is not volume.

The strongest community work is often modest. It notices where people pause, writes for that exact moment, and resists turning every paragraph into a campaign.

Base, plainly

A place where trying things can feel less expensive and less intimidating.

Base matters because lower-friction environments change behavior. When an action feels less costly, people can learn by doing: open a wallet, read a transaction, try a small app, ask a better question.

That does not make the ecosystem simple by default. It makes the need for careful explanation more visible. The page before the click still matters.

Field guide

Four small formats worth making.

Thread

One term, one example.

A short explanation of gas, bridging, or wallets with no assumptions about what the reader already knows.

Diagram

The path before the action.

A simple visual route from wallet to app to transaction, drawn like notes in the margin rather than a product graphic.

Essay

A story from inside the feed.

What a person tried, where they got stuck, and the one sentence that would have helped earlier.

Checklist

Slow down the click.

A calm list of what to read before connecting, minting, swapping, or sending anything anywhere.

Closing note

The point is not to make Base sound bigger. It is to make it easier to enter carefully.

BORF can be useful when it behaves like a good margin note: concise, specific, and close to the reader’s actual question.
